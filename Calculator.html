Euler Turbomachinery Equation

The Euler Turbomachinery equation states that the work transfer in a rotor is related to the product of the change in gas tangential velocity and the blade speed. Assuming that there is no change in the mean velocity radius and and the flow is purely axial, the equation is give by:

W/m = U(Cθ2 - Cθ3)

Using relations for from the velocity triangles, the equation can be expressed as:

W/m = UCa(tanβ2 - tanβ3)

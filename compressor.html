Velocity Triangles: Compressors

A single compressor stage consists of first a rotor stage then the stator stage. The passage ducts in the rotor blade row have an increasing area. Work is done on the air by the rotor blade, which in turn increases the stagnation pressure of the air. The fluid is then diffused in the stator blade passage, to reduce the velocity and increase the static pressure.

Example

At the inlet to a compressor stage, the absolute flow velocity is measured to be 250 m/s at an angle of 17 degrees to the axis. If the blade speed is 350 m/s calculate the velocity and flow angles relative to teh rotor blades.

Step 1: List all the variables that the question gives.

C1: 250 m/s
α1 : 17o
U: 350 m/s

Step 2: Identify the variables that the question wants you to answer.

W1
β1

Step 3: Draw velocity traiangles for the compressor.

Step 4: Calculate the value of the tangential component of absolute velocity (Cθ), and calculate the tangential component of the relative velocity (Wθ)

Cθ1 = C1sin(17)
Cθ1 = 73.1o

Wθ1 = U - Cθ1
Wθ1 = 279.9 m/s

Step 5: Calculate the axial velocity (Ca, this can usually be assumed be constant throughout the stage).

Ca = C1cos(17)
Ca = 239.1 m/s

Step 6: Use trigonometry to calulate the avlues for W1 and β1

W1 = (Ca2 + Wθ12)1/2
W1 = 368.1 m/s

β1 = tan-1 ( Wθ1 / Ca )
β1 = 49.9o

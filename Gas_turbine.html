Gas Turbines

Gas turbines are often used to power most aircrafts, due to it's relative light-weight design, efficiency and amount power output. They can also be used for the generation of electricity. Air is first taken in though the inlet. The majority of the air bypasses the core engine and is compressed and accelerated out through the by-pass nozzle. The majority of the propulsion comes from the air by-passing the main engine. The remaining air is passed through a series of compressors to increase the pressure of the air. The air is then mixed with fuel in the combustion chamber and ingnited. The resultant combustion gases are then passed though a series of turbine stages, where the exhaust gas expands.
